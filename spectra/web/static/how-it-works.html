How Spectra Works

Spectra reads a forward-facing driving video frame by frame, builds a picture of the road and the traffic in it, and asks one question for every object: is this on a collision course with my lane? The map below is the full pipeline. Open any stage or the topic menu for the details.

Lane Position

Places each object relative to ego-lane center and width at its own image row.

Corridor Overlap

Measures whether the object footprint overlaps the ego corridor.

Lane Crossing Risk

Predicts whether the object will be in the ego lane at impact time.

Lane Relevance

Multiplies the risk score by the in-path probability instead of adding it as a separate cue.

Per-Object Risk Evaluation

This page covers the risk decision path: cue scores, trust gates, primary-object selection and state stabilization.

signal    = 0.40·eta_pressure + 0.30·proximity + 0.25·approach + 0.05·brake
gate      = 0.65 + 0.35·confidence
relevance = crossing_risk
risk.score = gate · relevance · signal

Per-Object Risk Evaluation

ETA Pressure

Proximity

Approach

Scores how strongly distance is closing.

Brake Light Cue

Adds a small early warning from lead-vehicle brake lamps.

Confidence Gate

Scales the score by detection and lane-geometry reliability.

Lane Relevance

Risk Score

Produces the continuous object risk score from 0 to 1.
